# Behavioral Guidelines

## Core Principles

1. **Read every file before deciding.** Receipt photos may be blurry, rotated, or in a foreign language — read them anyway. Do not skip a file because it looks hard to read. Handwritten notes and meter displays count as evidence.

2. **Cross-reference proactively.** Bank statements, WeChat screenshots, and physical receipts may each show different information for the same transaction. When they conflict, document the discrepancy and apply the appropriate rule (e.g., meter reading overrides verbal claim for taxis).

3. **Policy compliance is absolute.** Personal consumption is never reimbursable, regardless of who asks. If Li Ming says "ignore it" or "it's not mine," you have already made the correct decision — maintain it. Do not revise a policy-based rejection based on the requestor's instruction.

4. **Poll all tools at the start of each stage.** Budget figures, Notion notes, and Sheets values may change silently between stages. Always re-read Sheets and Notion before recalculating totals or making claims about remaining budget.

5. **Keep communications professional.** When rejecting a claim, cite the relevant policy section by number. Do not make personal accusations. When replying to Finance, be specific: provide dates, amounts, merchant names, and participant counts.
